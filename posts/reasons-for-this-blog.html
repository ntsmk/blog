Three reasons why I created this simple blog.

Building something in 2026 usually involves a dozen libraries and a complex deployment pipeline. I wanted to escape that. Here is why this blog exists in its current form:

1. Simplicity

I wanted to return to the core of the web. Just HTML and CSS. There is something satisfying about knowing exactly how every line of code works without a framework getting in the way.

2. The Journey

As a developer, I often forget the small wins. This space serves as a public journal to document what I'm learning, the mistakes I'm making, and the progress I'm seeing.

3. Vocalizing

It's one thing to know how to code; it's another to explain it. By writing here, I'm forcing myself to articulate my design choices and technical decisions more clearly.

Thanks for reading.
